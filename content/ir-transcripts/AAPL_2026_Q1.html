Kevan Parekh — CFO

Thank you, Tim. Good afternoon, everyone. Let me walk you through our financial results in more detail.

Revenue for the quarter was $124.3 billion, up 4% year over year. Diluted EPS came in at $2.40 on a non-GAAP basis, above analyst forecasts of $2.35. Gross margin was 46.9%, a new all-time record for Apple in any quarter.

Turning to our segment results. Services revenue reached $26.3 billion, up 14% year over year — an all-time record. iPhone revenue was $69.1 billion, in line with expectations. Mac delivered $9.0 billion, iPad $8.1 billion, and Wearables, Home & Accessories came in at $11.7 billion.

Greater China revenue was $18.5 billion, down 11% year over year, reflecting ongoing competitive pressures in that region.

Regarding capital allocation: we announced a $30 billion share buyback program and raised our quarterly dividend to $0.25 per share. Total capital returned to shareholders year to date reached $28.3 billion.

For Q2 2026, we are guiding revenue in the range of $88.5 billion to $91.5 billion, and gross margin between 46.5% and 47.5%. Operating expenses are expected to be between $15.3 billion and $15.5 billion.
